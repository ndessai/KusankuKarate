About Shihan Vu Nguyen

Shihan Vu Nguyen is the founder of Kusanku Karate which is a school of Zenkoku Ryukyu Kempo Karate-Do Renmei under Hanshi Albert Geraldi. Shihan Vu began his karate training in 1998 at Dowling College under Sensei Kim and Sensei Glenn. In 2005 the club was dissolved and he then continues his training with Kyoshi Kenneth LaPeter.

Shihan Vu is currently a Godan (fourth degree black belt) and continues to train to further develop his karate skills. He shares his knowledge freely with his students. Shihan Vu loves teaching karate to children, especially those with special needs and disabilities. His classes are fun, energetic and informative.

History

Japan is made up of a series of island. The lower west portions of the islands are called the Ryukyu islands including Okinawa. The karate that is practiced there was taught to Seiyu Oyata through his father. During world war two he learned a number of forms of karate including judo, kendo, and chinese martial arts.

Most martial arts were practiced in public but Seiyu learned from Uhugushiku and Wakinaguri who taught him arts passed down from generation to generation and kept hidden. Later on Seiyu studied under Shigeru Nakamura founder of Kenpo and learned the basic twelve empty hand katas. He came to America to teach his arts in 1977 and taught Albert Geraldi, Jim Logue, Bill Wiswell and Greg Lindquest. eiyu Oyata's karate iscalled Ryu-te which means flowing hand (like the islands).

The school we study in is Zenkoku Ryukyu Kempo Karate-Do Renmei founded by Hanshi Albert Geraldi.
